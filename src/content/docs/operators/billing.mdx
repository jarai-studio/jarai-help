JARAI bills on a **subscription + token allowance** model. Each AI call your productions
make (text, image generation, voiceover, video composition, music…) consumes a budgeted
number of **tokens** from your monthly allowance. This page explains tiers, allowances,
and how to keep producing when you run low.

## Tiers

| Tier | For | Allowance | Accounts |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Creator** | Individuals getting started | Entry monthly token allowance | Single account |
| **Pro** | Regular producers | Larger allowance + priority processing | Unlimited accounts |
| **Atelier** | Teams | Pooled capacity across seats | Multi-seat team billing |

See current pricing on [jarai.studio](https://www.jarai.studio). Annual prepay carries
a discount over monthly.

## Token allowances

Each AI call consumes a budgeted number of **tokens**. Heavier steps (video
composition, long voiceover) cost more than light ones (a short text generation). Your
**monthly allowance** refreshes each billing cycle.

## The usage banner

The Console shows a **billing banner** with your remaining allowance. When you reach
about **75%** of the monthly allowance with auto-top-up off, the banner prompts you to
**buy extra capacity** so productions don't stall mid-month.

<Aside type="tip">
Turn on **auto-top-up** if you'd rather not be interrupted — JARAI adds capacity
automatically when you run low, and you keep producing without watching the banner.
</Aside>

## Extra capacity (top-ups)

When you need more than your tier includes, buy **extra capacity** on top of the
subscription. It's added to your balance and consumed after the monthly allowance.

## Budgets & limits

Accounts can carry a **production budget**. If an account hits its monthly limit, new
productions are **rejected up front** with a clear "budget limit reached" message
rather than partially running. Raise the budget or buy capacity to resume.

## Seeing where the money goes

The **Account P&L** dashboard shows revenue, AI cost, and margin for an account; the
**Customer P&L** rolls it up across a customer's accounts. Use these to see which
productions or steps are driving cost.

<Aside type="note">
Partner API usage draws on the **same** allowance and billing as the Console — there
isn't a separate API meter.
</Aside>
