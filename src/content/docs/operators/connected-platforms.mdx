To publish to your channels, JARAI needs permission to post on your behalf. You grant
that once per channel via **OAuth** under **Settings → Connected platforms**. After
that, approving a production to publish fans it out to your connected channels
automatically.

<VideoHelper src="connected-platforms.mp4" title="Connect a publishing channel">

1. Open **Settings → Connected platforms**.
2. Click **Connect** next to a platform (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, Facebook…).
3. Complete the platform's OAuth consent in the popup.
4. The channel shows as **Connected** — approved productions can now publish to it.

</VideoHelper>

## Connecting a channel

<Steps>

1. **Click Connect** for the platform. You're redirected to that platform's own consent screen.

2. **Grant the requested scopes.** JARAI asks only for what it needs to publish and read back basic engagement.

3. **Return to the Console.** The platform now shows **Connected**, with the account/handle it's linked to.

</Steps>

## How publishing uses connections

When you **Approve to publish** a finished production (see [Distribution & publishing](/operators/distribution/)),
JARAI's distribution loop posts to each connected channel that's in scope, then tracks
the published URLs and engagement back into the **Distribution** dashboard.

## Tokens, expiry & reconnecting

OAuth access is token-based and tokens **expire** or get **revoked** by the platform.
JARAI refreshes them automatically where the platform allows. If a channel shows
**Disconnected** or **Needs attention**:

<Steps>

1. **Reconnect** — click Connect again and re-grant consent. This is the fix for an expired/revoked token.

2. **Check platform-side access** — if you removed JARAI's app authorisation on the platform, you'll need to re-authorise.

</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Per-platform availability">
Some platforms require app review/approval before production posting is enabled. If a
connect option is missing or limited, that platform's integration may still be
pending approval — content for it is held rather than failing.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note">
Connections are managed per account. Disconnecting a channel stops future
publications to it but doesn't remove already-published content.
</Aside>
