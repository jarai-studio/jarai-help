A **voice variant** is a single TTS voice from one of JARAI's speech providers. The
catalogue spans many providers, languages, and accents, and you pick a variant per
[avatar](/operators/avatars/). This page covers how to find the right voice.

<VideoHelper src="voice-variants.mp4" title="Find the right voice" duration="90 sec">

1. Open the voice picker (on an avatar) or **Settings → Voice variants** to browse.
2. Use the **trait chips** to filter — accent, age, gender, style, use-case.
3. **Preview** a variant with the inline player.
4. Pick it for your avatar; if the accent you want has no voice yet, request it.

</VideoHelper>

## Filtering by traits

Every voice carries provider metadata parsed into **trait facets** you can filter by:

- **Accent** — the regional accent (see below).
- **Age**, **Gender**, **Style** — broad voice character.
- **Use cases** — what the provider suggests the voice suits (narration, ads, audiobook…).
- **Tags / colour** — extra descriptors where the provider supplies them.

Select chips across categories to narrow down: filtering is **AND across categories**
(accent *and* gender) and **OR within a category** (British *or* Irish). The same
filter appears here and in the avatar voice picker, so a selection drills to the same
voices in both places.

## Accents

Accent is a first-class trait. Voices are tagged with a precise regional label — from
country level (e.g. *Spain — Castilian*, *UK — Received Pronunciation*) down to
sub-regional accents (e.g. *Andalusia — Sevillano*, *Brazil — Carioca*). Use the
accent filter to match a voice to your audience.

<Aside type="tip">
The accent catalogue is intentionally deep. If you produce for a specific region,
filtering by its accent makes the output feel local rather than generic.
</Aside>

## Previewing

Use the inline **preview player** before committing a voice to an avatar — a voice
that reads well for ads may not suit long-form narration, and accent nuance is easier
to hear than to read.

## When there's no voice for an accent yet

The accent taxonomy is broader than any single provider's catalogue, so some accents
show as **available to request** rather than ready to use. If the accent you need has
no voice:

- Pick the closest available accent (often the parent country/region), **or**
- [Contact support](/general/contact-support/) to request the specific accent — demand signal helps prioritise which voices get added.

<Aside type="note">
New voices are added over time as providers expand and as the voice harvester
discovers and vets them, so the catalogue you see grows.
</Aside>
