Each account carries a set of **policies** that tell the pipeline how to behave for that
account — how to source images and video, when to require captions, whether to label AI
content, how to engage with audiences, and how to run promotion. You edit them from the
account page; each policy opens as its own form. Operators and platform admins can edit;
customer roles see them **read-only**.

<VideoHelper src="account-policies.mp4" title="Set an account policy">

1. Open the account, then pick a **policy card** (Accessibility, Image acquisition, …).
2. Adjust the settings — each policy has a **Policy active** switch plus its own fields.
3. **Save.** The change applies to the account's *next* productions.

</VideoHelper>

## The policies at a glance

Eight policies always apply; two more appear only for accounts using the relevant theme.

| Policy | Governs | Key controls |
|---|---|---|
| **Accessibility** | WCAG conformance of deliverables | Standard (WCAG 2.1 AA/AAA, 508, EN 301 549), require captions / audio-description / transcript, minimum caption accuracy |
| **Image acquisition** | How images are sourced | Strategy (Stock / AI / Operator / Hybrid), stock library priority, safety + alignment thresholds, copyright-risk auto-reject |
| **Video acquisition** | How footage is sourced | Strategy (Stock / AI text-to-video / image-to-video / Broadcast feed / Hybrid), library priority, avatar animation |
| **Content risk** | Brand-safety guardrails | Flag public figures / logos / mature content, allow identifiable real people (adds a consent gate) |
| **Content disclosure** | AI-authorship labelling | Require disclosure, text, position (intro / outro / overlay), format (text card / spoken / both), what it applies to |
| **Financial services** | FCA compliance for regulated content | FCA audience classification, mandatory pre-publish review, financial-content guardrail |
| **Promotion** | Loop 5 paid + organic promotion | Enable paid campaigns, budget caps, promotion window, monetisation metric, platform scope |
| **Engagement** | Loop 3 audience interaction | Which interaction types to answer, tone, auto-post vs review, daily limits, AI-reply disclosure |
| **Talks production** *(Talks accounts)* | The look + rules of Talks debates | Speaker balance, composition style (split-screen / alternating / reaction), consistency + coherence scores, caption format |
| **Historical image animation** *(Time-Travel accounts)* | Animating archival imagery | Image source (AI / Wikimedia), colourisation, motion effect, animation focus |

## What each policy does

### Accessibility
Sets the **accessibility standard** to target and whether deliverables must include
**captions**, **audio descriptions** and **transcripts** — each toggle activates the
matching pipeline step. You also set a **minimum caption accuracy** (default 0.98) and can
require **human review** of audio descriptions before the production clears its gate. See
the platform [Accessibility statement](/general/accessibility/).

### Image acquisition & Video acquisition
Choose the **sourcing strategy** — stock libraries, AI generation, operator-supplied, or a
hybrid — and the **priority order** of stock libraries. Acquisition enforces a minimum
**content-safety** and **prompt-alignment** score, can **auto-reject high copyright-risk**
images, and falls back to AI generation when allowed. Video acquisition adds clip-length
and cost caps and an **avatar animation** option.

### Content risk
Brand-safety guardrails: flag **identifiable public figures**, **brand logos** and **mature
content**. Allowing **identifiable real people** is off by default and, when enabled, adds a
consent/approval gate before publish.

### Content disclosure
Controls whether productions carry an **AI-authorship disclosure**, and if so the **text**,
**position** (intro, outro, or persistent overlay) and **format** (on-screen card, spoken,
or both) — relevant to FTC guidance and EU AI Act Article 50.

### Financial services
For regulated finance content, set the **FCA audience classification**. When it's anything
other than *Not applicable*, you can require a **mandatory compliance review** before
distribution and keep the **financial-content guardrail** on (strongly recommended).

### Promotion
Governs **Loop 5**. With paid promotion off, JARAI promotes organically only. Turn it on to
set a **budget cap per production**, a **promotion window**, the **monetisation metric**
(views, watch-time, streams, revenue), and an optional **platform scope** and per-platform
budget overrides.

### Engagement
Governs **Loop 3**. Pick which **interaction types** to respond to, the **tone**, and whether
replies **auto-post** or wait for operator review. Daily caps on responses and sentiment
analyses keep spend bounded, and you can append an **AI-reply disclosure**.

### Talks production & Historical image animation
These appear only when the account uses the matching theme. **Talks production** sets speaker
balance, the **composition style** (split-screen, alternating, reaction shots), quality
thresholds and caption format — see [Talks queue](/operators/talks-queue/). **Historical
image animation** sets the archival-image source (AI or Wikimedia, which requires
attribution), colourisation, and the motion style.

<Aside type="note">
Every policy has a **Policy active** switch. Turn a policy off and JARAI falls back to safe
platform defaults (e.g. all content-risk flags on, real-person content disallowed). Policy
changes apply to the account's **next** productions, not ones already running.
</Aside>

<Aside type="tip">
Customer roles (CustomerAdministrator, CustomerViewer) can **see** policies but not change
them — they're set by the operator or platform admin who runs the account.
</Aside>
