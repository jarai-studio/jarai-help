When a production is ready, it appears in your Client Portal for review. **Nothing is
published until you approve it** — you're the final say on what goes out.

<VideoHelper src="/videos/reviewing.mp4" title="Review and approve your content" duration="90 sec">

1. Open the production from your list (or from the [notification](/customers/notifications/)).
2. **Preview** every asset — video, audio, captions.
3. Check the **rights review** — acknowledge any advisory warnings.
4. **Approve** to publish, or **Reject** to send it back.

</VideoHelper>

## Finding productions to review

Productions awaiting your decision are highlighted in the portal and you're notified
when one is ready (see [Notifications](/customers/notifications/)). Open one to start
reviewing.

## Previewing

<Steps>

1. **Play the video / audio** inline and read the **captions/transcript**. Preview the platform variants if more than one was produced.

2. **Read the details** — the topic, the avatar(s) used, and any per-platform metadata (title, description).

3. **Check the rights review** — JARAI surfaces advisory warnings (copyright, likeness, attribution) per platform. Acknowledge them so you're publishing with the risks visible. Full detail is on [Approval and rights review](/customers/rights-review/).

</Steps>

## Approving or rejecting

- **Approve** → the production is published to the connected channels in scope, and you can track results.
- **Reject** → the production doesn't publish. Use reject when the content isn't right; your operator can adjust and re-run.
- **Per-platform choice** → you can approve for some channels and not others where that option is offered.

<Aside type="tip">
Approvals are time-sensitive — content waits for your decision. If several people on
your side review, agree who owns the final approval so productions don't sit waiting.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note">
Approving is a recorded decision (who approved, when, and what warnings were
acknowledged) — this protects you and provides an audit trail for what was published.
</Aside>
