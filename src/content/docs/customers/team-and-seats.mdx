More than one person can use your JARAI account, at different access levels. If you're
on a team plan, capacity is shared across everyone. This page explains the roles and
how seats work.

<VideoHelper title="Manage your team" duration="60 sec">

1. As a **Customer administrator**, open team management in the Client Portal.
2. Invite a colleague by email and pick their role.
3. **Administrator** can manage and approve; **Viewer** is read-only.
4. On the **Atelier** plan, capacity pools across all seats.

## Roles

| Role | Can do |
|---|---|
| **Customer administrator** | See everything for the customer, approve/reject productions, manage billing, manage team members |
| **Customer viewer** | Read-only — see productions and status, but not approve or change billing |

Give people the **least access they need**: most stakeholders only need **Viewer**;
reserve **Administrator** for those who approve content or manage billing.

## Pooled capacity (Atelier)

The **Atelier** plan is built for teams: instead of a per-person allowance, capacity
**pools** across all seats. More seats means more shared capacity, drawn from one
allowance. Individual members still have their own logins and visibility, but they
spend from the shared pool.

<Aside type="tip">
If your team keeps hitting the allowance, adding seats on Atelier raises the shared
pool — see [Billing and subscriptions](/customers/billing/) for plan details, and the
[pricing on jarai.studio](https://www.jarai.studio) for current tiers.
</Aside>

## Adding & removing people

- **Invite** a colleague by email; they get access at the role you choose.
- **Change a role** as responsibilities shift (e.g. promote a Viewer to Administrator).
- **Remove** someone when they leave — their access ends immediately; content they were involved with is unaffected.

<Aside type="caution">
Keep at least one **Customer administrator** at all times — if the last admin is
removed, no one can approve content or manage the team. Contact
[support](/general/contact-support/) if you're locked out.
</Aside>
